# NotebookLM Prompt Example

## Prompt

Audience: graduate students with introductory familiarity with quantum many-body physics and quantum information.
Duration: about 20 minutes.

Build the deck around this story:
Show that this paper is not just about unusual nonthermal states, but about why those states may be useful for work extraction. Introduce ergotropy as the usable-work lens, then explain why many-body scar states in the PXP model are special because they stay low-entanglement compared with thermal states. Use the scar-to-thermal crossover to connect entanglement structure with extractable work. End by showing that the coherent-rotation protocol turns this idea into a concrete quantum-battery-style scenario.

Narrative priorities:

- Foreground ergotropy as the physical quantity that turns nonthermal dynamics into a work-extraction question.
- Treat entanglement growth as the central obstacle that separates scar-like behavior from thermal behavior.
- Use the PXP model as the main testbed rather than as background formalism.
- Spend the most time on the entanglement-ergotropy anti-correlation and the coherent-rotation protocol.
- Keep the tone as proof-of-principle: promising for quantum battery intuition, but not a complete device proposal.

Required coverage:
1. Why many-body scars matter beyond nonthermality
2. Thermal states versus scar states in one intuitive comparison
3. What ergotropy means physically as extractable work
4. The central hypothesis: low entanglement supports higher ergotropy
5. The PXP model as the paper's testbed
6. The eigenstate crossover and entanglement-ergotropy anti-correlation as the main conceptual result
7. The coherent-rotation charging and extraction protocol
8. Why the dynamical result matters for quantum battery intuition
9. The paper's key limitation or open question

Evidence to show:

- The paper's eigenstate crossover evidence connecting scar-like and thermal regimes
- The entanglement versus ergotropy comparison that anchors the main conceptual claim
- Multipartite entanglement diagnostics used as supporting evidence
- The coherent-rotation charging and extraction protocol result

Image handling:

- Recreate important paper figures at presentation quality when the original figure is too dense.
- Keep redraws structurally faithful to the axes, regimes, and comparisons in the paper.
- Explain what each figure proves before moving to the next claim.
- Use diagrams and figures as evidence, not as decorative background.

Visual style:
- white background
- black text
- colorful figures and diagrams where helpful
- clean academic presentation style
- one key idea per slide
- use paper figures or paper-faithful redraws when possible
- prioritize image clarity over ornamental layout
