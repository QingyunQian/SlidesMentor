# NotebookLM Prompt

## Prompt

Create a teaching-oriented slide deck for this paper.

Audience: graduate students with introductory familiarity with quantum many-body physics and quantum information.
Duration: about 20 minutes.

Build the deck around this teaching story:
Show that this paper is not just about unusual nonthermal states, but about why those states may be useful for work extraction. Introduce ergotropy as the usable-work lens, then explain why many-body scar states in the PXP model are special because they stay low-entanglement compared with thermal states. Use the scar-to-thermal crossover to connect entanglement structure with extractable work. End by showing that the coherent-rotation protocol turns this idea into a concrete quantum-battery-style scenario.

Required coverage:
1. Why many-body scars matter beyond nonthermality
2. Thermal states versus scar states in one intuitive comparison
3. What ergotropy means physically as extractable work
4. The central hypothesis: low entanglement supports higher ergotropy
5. The PXP model as the paper’s testbed
6. The eigenstate crossover from scar regime to thermal regime
7. The entanglement-ergotropy anti-correlation as the main conceptual result
8. The supporting multipartite entanglement evidence
9. The coherent-rotation charging and extraction protocol
10. Why the dynamical result matters for quantum battery intuition
11. The main takeaway for quantum thermodynamics and battery design
12. The paper’s key limitation or open question

Visual style:
- white background
- black text
- colorful figures and diagrams where helpful
- clean academic presentation style
- one key idea per slide

Do NOT include:
- no long paragraphs
- do not restate the abstract as slides
- no more than one core idea per slide
- avoid dense equations unless essential
